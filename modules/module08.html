Smart Money Concepts

A framework for thinking about price action as a record of where larger participants are likely positioned.

Liquidity

Buy-side liquidity sits above old highs (stop-losses of short sellers, breakout buy orders). Sell-side liquidity sits below old lows. Price is often drawn toward these pools before reversing.

Animation — Liquidity sweep above equal highs, then reversal

Order Blocks

An order block is the last opposing candle before a strong impulsive move — treated as a zone of likely institutional interest on retest.

Animation — Order block identification & retest

Fair Value Gap (FVG)

A three-candle imbalance where the middle candle moves so fast it leaves a visible gap between candle 1's wick and candle 3's wick — often a magnet for price to return and "fill."

Animation — FVG formation and fill

Equal Highs/Lows, Premium & Discount, Inducement

  • Equal highs/lows mark obvious liquidity pools.
  • Premium/discount splits a recent range at its midpoint.
  • Inducement is a deliberate-looking small move designed to trap traders before the real move.
