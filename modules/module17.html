Algorithmic Trading & Backtesting

Testing strategies, avoiding overfitting, and accounting for realistic friction.

What a Backtest Is

Running a strategy's exact rules against historical data to see how it would have performed — useful only to the extent conditions realistically resemble live trading.

Overfitting — The Biggest Backtest Trap

Tuning parameters until a strategy matches the noise in one dataset, rather than capturing a real repeatable edge. Fewer parameters + larger trade sample = less overfitting risk.

Simulator — In-sample fit vs out-of-sample degradation

Walk-Forward Validation

Tune parameters on one period (in-sample), then test unchanged on a later period (out-of-sample). If performance collapses, the strategy was likely fit to noise.

Slippage & Realistic Friction

Backtests assuming perfect fills overstate performance. Build in realistic slippage and transaction costs before trusting numbers.

Position Sizing & Portfolio Heat

Even a sound edge ruins an account with aggressive sizing. Cap total simultaneous open risk ("portfolio heat") regardless of signal confidence.
