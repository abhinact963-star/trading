Trend Analysis

The most useful thing you can know as a trader: which direction is price heading right now?

Uptrend — Price Is Climbing Like Stairs

In an uptrend, price keeps making new highs and new lows — but each new high is HIGHER than the last high, and each new low is HIGHER than the last low. Imagine climbing stairs. Each step is higher than the one before. Even the small steps backward (pullbacks) don't go all the way down. Traders call these Higher Highs (HH) and Higher Lows (HL).

Animation — Uptrend: HH / HL sequence building

Downtrend — Price Is Falling Like Stairs Going Down

In a downtrend, it is the opposite. Each new high is LOWER than the last. Each new low is LOWER than the last. Like stairs going downhill. Even the bounces upward are weak — they never recover to the previous high. Traders call these Lower Highs (LH) and Lower Lows (LL).

Animation — Downtrend: LH / LL sequence

Sideways — Price Is Going Nowhere

Sometimes price just bounces back and forth between two levels — a top and a bottom. It doesn't go up or down in any clear direction. Like a ball bouncing in a box. Traders call this a range or a sideways market. Most professional traders either avoid trading in a range or wait for price to break out of it first.
