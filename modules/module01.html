Trading Fundamentals

What a market actually is, who you are trading against, and the vocabulary everything else builds on.

What is Trading?

Buying and selling an asset with the intent of profiting from a change in its price — distinct from investing, which is usually longer-horizon and ownership-driven.

Markets You Can Trade

  • Stocks Ownership stakes in companies, traded on exchanges like NSE/BSE.
  • Forex Currency pairs, the largest and most liquid market, open 24/5.
  • Crypto Digital assets, trades 24/7, typically higher volatility.

Types of Traders (by holding time)

  • Scalper — seconds to minutes, many small trades.
  • Day trader — opens and closes within the same session.
  • Swing trader — days to weeks, rides a directional move.
  • Position trader — weeks to months, closer to investing.

Bid, Ask, and the Spread

The bid is the highest price a buyer will pay right now. The ask is the lowest price a seller will accept. The gap between them is the spread — effectively the cost of immediacy.

Leverage and Margin

Leverage lets you control a larger position than your capital alone would allow, by borrowing the rest. Margin is the capital you must keep aside as collateral. Leverage amplifies gains exactly as much as it amplifies losses — it does not change the underlying odds.
