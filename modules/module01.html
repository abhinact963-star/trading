Trading Fundamentals

Start here. You'll learn what trading is, what you can trade, and the basic words you'll hear every day.

What is Trading?

Trading is simple. You buy something at one price and sell it at a higher price. The difference is your profit. For example: you buy a share of Reliance for ₹2,400. Later it goes to ₹2,500. You sell. You made ₹100. That's trading. It's different from investing — investors hold for years. Traders hold for minutes, hours, or days.

What Can You Trade?

  • Stocks Shares in companies like Reliance, TCS, Infosys. You buy a tiny piece of the company.
  • Forex Currencies like USD/INR (Dollar vs Rupee). You bet on which currency gets stronger.
  • Crypto Digital coins like Bitcoin. It trades 24/7. Prices move a lot — more risk, more chance of profit.

What Kind of Trader Are You?

  • Scalper — You enter and exit in seconds or minutes. You take many tiny profits per day.
  • Day Trader — You open and close all trades before the market closes. You never hold overnight.
  • Swing Trader — You hold a trade for a few days or weeks to catch one big move.
  • Position Trader — You hold for months. Very slow. Almost like investing.

Bid, Ask, and Spread — The Cost of Buying

Imagine you want to buy mangoes. The seller wants ₹100 per kg. You want to pay ₹98. The gap between what you will pay and what the seller wants is the spread. In trading: the bid is what buyers will pay right now. The ask is what sellers want right now. Every time you trade, you pay the spread. Smaller spread = cheaper to trade.

Leverage — Trading With Borrowed Money

Leverage lets you control a bigger trade than the money you actually have. With 10x leverage, ₹10,000 of your own money controls ₹1,00,000 of a trade. Sounds great — but here's the catch: if the trade goes your way, you make 10x more. If it goes against you, you lose 10x more. Leverage does NOT make bad trades good. It just makes everything bigger. Use it carefully.
