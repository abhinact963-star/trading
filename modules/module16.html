Options Trading & Volatility

Calls, puts, Greeks, IV — essential for anyone trading index derivatives.

Calls, Puts, and Premium

A call gives the right to buy at a fixed strike. A put gives the right to sell. The premium is the price paid for that right.

Implied Volatility (IV) and IV Rank

IV is the market's estimate of future movement baked into option prices. IV Rank tells you whether current IV is high or low relative to its own past year.

The Greeks

  • Delta — price change per 1-unit move in underlying.
  • Theta — daily value lost from time decay (sellers earn it, buyers pay it).
  • Gamma — how fast delta changes, accelerates near expiry.
  • Vega — sensitivity to IV change.

Buying vs. Selling Premium

Buying has defined risk but fights theta daily and is vulnerable to IV crush. Selling collects theta but carries larger risk unless hedged.

Credit Spreads

Sell one option, buy a further-out hedge. Caps max loss at strike difference minus premium collected.
