Fibonacci

A set of price levels that millions of traders around the world watch. Because so many people watch them, price often respects them.

Fibonacci Retracement — Where Will the Pullback Stop?

After a strong move up, price often pulls back before continuing higher. The question is: how far will it pull back? Fibonacci retracement levels give you likely stopping points. You draw them from the start of the move to the end. The key levels are 23.6%, 38.2%, 50%, 61.8%, and 78.6%. The 61.8% level is the most famous — it is called the "Golden Ratio" and is the most watched level globally.

Fibonacci Extension — Where Will the Move Go Next?

Once a pullback finishes and price starts moving up again, where will it go? Fibonacci extensions project targets beyond the original move. Common extension levels are 127.2% and 161.8%. You use these as potential take-profit targets rather than entry points.

Be Honest: Fibonacci Is Not Magic

Fibonacci levels are not guaranteed. They work partly because so many traders watch the same levels — so price sometimes bounces just because a lot of buy orders are sitting there. But they don't always work. Use Fibonacci as one clue — not as the whole reason for a trade. A Fibonacci level that also lines up with a support zone AND a key moving average is MUCH stronger than Fibonacci alone.
