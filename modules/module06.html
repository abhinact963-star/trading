Market Structure

How to know if a trend is continuing — or if it's about to change direction.

The Four Building Blocks

Every move on a chart can be described with just four labels. HH = Higher High (new peak above the last). HL = Higher Low (a dip that stops higher than the last dip). LH = Lower High (a bounce that fails below the last bounce). LL = Lower Low (a drop that goes below the last drop). These four labels describe every trend.

Break of Structure — The Trend Is Still Alive

A Break of Structure (BOS) happens when price pushes past the last peak in an uptrend — making a new Higher High. This confirms: the uptrend is still going. It's just the market saying "we're still climbing." In a downtrend, a BOS happens when price breaks below the last low. The trend continues.

Animation — BOS in an uptrend (breaking above prior HH)

Change of Character — First Warning Something Has Changed

A Change of Character (CHoCH) is the first sign a trend might be ending. In an uptrend, it happens when price breaks BELOW the last Higher Low for the first time. It's not a reversal yet — but it's a warning. Like hearing a strange sound in your car. You don't stop immediately, but you pay closer attention.

Animation — CHoCH: uptrend HL broken, first bearish signal

Market Structure Shift — Reversal Confirmed

A Market Structure Shift (MSS) is stronger confirmation that the trend has actually reversed. After a CHoCH, if price starts making Lower Highs and Lower Lows, the old uptrend is dead. A new downtrend has begun. Now you trade in the new direction, not the old one.

Animation — MSS: full trend reversal confirmed

Big Moves Have Smaller Moves Inside Them

Zoom out and you see a big trend. Zoom in and you see smaller waves within it. The big waves are the main trend. The small waves inside are the internal moves. Smart traders use the small internal moves to find better spots to enter the bigger trend trade.
