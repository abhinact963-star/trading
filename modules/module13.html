Risk Management

The part of trading that determines whether you survive long enough for your edge to matter.

Position Sizing

How large a position to take, usually expressed as a fixed percentage of account risked per trade (commonly 1-2%) — keeping risk proportional as account size changes.

Stop-Loss & Take-Profit

A stop-loss defines maximum acceptable loss. A take-profit defines where gains are locked in. Both should be set before entry.

Risk:Reward Ratio

The ratio between what you stand to lose (entry to stop) vs gain (entry to target). A 1:2 RR means risking 1 to potentially make 2 — it lets you be profitable even with sub-50% win rate.

Maximum Drawdown

The largest peak-to-trough decline an account has experienced. Small per-trade loss can still produce large drawdown if losses cluster — portfolio-level risk caps matter.
