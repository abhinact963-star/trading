Technical Analysis: Lines & Chart Patterns

Shapes that appear on charts again and again. Learn to recognise them and you'll know what's likely to happen next.

Trendlines and Channels

A trendline is a straight line you draw connecting two or more price lows (in an uptrend) or two or more price highs (in a downtrend). When price keeps touching that line and bouncing, the trend is still alive. Draw a parallel second line on the other side and you get a channel. Price bounces back and forth between the two lines like a ball inside a tube.

Bullish Patterns — Sign of a Coming Rise

  • Ascending Triangle The top is a flat ceiling (sellers defending). The bottom keeps rising (buyers getting stronger). Eventually buyers win and price breaks up.
  • Cup & Handle Price forms a big smooth U-shape (the cup), then a small dip to the right (the handle), then breaks up. Looks like a teacup from the side.
  • Bull Flag Price rallies hard, then drifts sideways or slightly down (catching breath), then rallies again. The first move is the flagpole, the pause is the flag.
Animation — Ascending Triangle forming and breaking out

Bearish Patterns — Sign of a Coming Fall

  • Descending Triangle The bottom is a flat floor (buyers defending). The top keeps falling (sellers getting stronger). Usually breaks down.
  • Bear Flag Price drops hard, then bounces slightly, then drops again. The bounce is the flag.
  • Head & Shoulders Three peaks — small, tall (the head), small. When the low between the peaks breaks, price usually drops significantly.
Animation — Head & Shoulders pattern forming

Neutral Patterns — Wait for the Breakout

Some patterns like the Symmetrical Triangle or Rectangle can break either up or down. Don't try to guess which way. Wait for a candle to close clearly OUTSIDE the pattern before you act. Guessing early is one of the most expensive mistakes in trading.
