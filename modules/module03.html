Candlestick Patterns

Reversal and continuation shapes that recur often enough to be worth knowing — treated as probabilities, not certainties.

Bullish Reversal Patterns

  • Hammer small body, long lower wick, found after a downtrend.
  • Bullish Engulfing a large bullish candle fully engulfs the prior bearish one.
  • Morning Star a three-candle bottoming sequence.
  • Piercing Pattern, Three White Soldiers
Animation — Hammer forming after a downtrend

Bearish Reversal Patterns

  • Shooting Star small body, long upper wick, after an uptrend.
  • Bearish Engulfing, Evening Star
  • Dark Cloud Cover, Three Black Crows
Animation — Bearish Engulfing pattern

Continuation / Indecision Patterns

  • Doji — open and close nearly equal, signals indecision.
  • Spinning Top — small body, wicks on both sides.
  • Rising/Falling Three Methods — a pause within an ongoing trend.
Animation — Pattern step-through (reversal forming)

The Honest Caveat

No candlestick pattern works in isolation reliably. Context — where it forms relative to support/resistance, trend, and volume — matters more than the shape itself.
