Candlestick Patterns

Certain candle shapes tell you "buyers are getting strong" or "sellers are taking over." Here are the most important ones.

Bullish Patterns — Buyers Are Coming Back

  • Hammer After a price drop, you see a candle with a tiny body at the top and a long lower wick. It means sellers pushed price way down, but buyers fought back and pushed it almost all the way back up. Possible sign the drop is over.
  • Bullish Engulfing A large green candle completely swallows the previous red candle. Buyers totally overwhelmed sellers in one go.
  • Morning Star Three candles: first a red candle, then a tiny uncertain candle, then a strong green candle. Looks like the sun rising after darkness — the bottom may be in.
Animation — Hammer forming after a downtrend

Bearish Patterns — Sellers Are Taking Over

  • Shooting Star After a price rise, you see a candle with a tiny body at the bottom and a long upper wick. Buyers pushed price way up, but sellers slammed it back down. Possible sign the rise is over.
  • Bearish Engulfing A large red candle swallows the previous green candle. Sellers completely took over.
  • Evening Star The opposite of Morning Star. Three candles that signal a top may be forming.
Animation — Bearish Engulfing pattern

Indecision Patterns — Nobody Wins

  • Doji — The candle opens and closes at almost the same price. It means buyers and sellers were equally matched. Nobody won. The market is undecided.
  • Spinning Top — A small body with wicks on both sides. Again, both sides tried and neither won decisively.
Animation — Pattern step-through (reversal forming)

Important: Where It Appears Matters More Than What It Looks Like

A Hammer after a long drop at a key support level is meaningful. The same Hammer in the middle of a chart with no context? Probably just noise. Always ask: where is this pattern forming? A pattern at the right place is powerful. A pattern in the wrong place is meaningless.
