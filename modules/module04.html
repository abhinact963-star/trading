Support & Resistance

The most important idea in all of trading. Every professional trader uses this every single day.

Support — The Floor That Holds Price Up

Support is a price level where price has fallen to and bounced back up multiple times. Think of it as a floor. Every time price touches that floor, buyers step in and push it back up. The more times price has bounced from that level, the stronger the floor is — and the more traders are watching it.

Animation — Drag the support line, watch price react

Resistance — The Ceiling That Holds Price Down

Resistance is the exact opposite. It is a price level where price has risen to and been pushed back down multiple times. Like a ceiling. Every time price hits that ceiling, sellers step in and push it back down. You can think of support and resistance as invisible walls that price keeps bouncing off.

Moving Support and Resistance

Support and resistance can also move with price. A rising trendline acts like a moving floor — price keeps bouncing off it as the trend goes up. A moving average (like the 20-day average) can also act as a moving support level. These are called dynamic support/resistance because they change as price moves.

Animation — Dynamic S/R (moving average as support)

The Flip — Old Ceiling Becomes New Floor

Here's a powerful thing that happens. When price finally breaks through a resistance level (ceiling), that old ceiling often becomes the new floor (support). Why? Traders who missed buying the first time now wait for price to come back to that level and buy the dip. This "flip" from resistance to support is one of the most reliable things on any chart.
