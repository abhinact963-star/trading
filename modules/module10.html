Indicators

Mathematical transforms of price that help quantify momentum, trend, and volatility.

Moving Averages (SMA & EMA)

SMA weights every price equally. EMA weights recent prices more heavily, so it turns faster. Common uses: trend direction, dynamic S/R, crossover signals.

Animation — Price with SMA(20) and EMA(20) overlaid

RSI (Relative Strength Index)

Oscillates 0-100 based on recent gains vs losses. Above 70 = overbought, below 30 = oversold — though in strong trends RSI can stay extreme longer than expected.

Animation — Price vs RSI, synced in real-time

MACD

The difference between two EMAs, plotted with a signal line. Crossovers and divergence from price are the two most common uses.

Bollinger Bands & ATR

Bollinger Bands plot a moving average with bands at a multiple of standard deviation — bands widen in high volatility, narrow in low. ATR measures volatility directly, often used for stop-loss sizing.

Animation — Bollinger Bands expanding and contracting

VWAP

Volume-Weighted Average Price — the average price weighted by volume. Used as a fairness benchmark and intraday bias line by institutional traders.
